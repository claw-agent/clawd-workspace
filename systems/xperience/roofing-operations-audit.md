# Roofing Operations Audit Framework
## 9-Domain Discovery & Automation Roadmap

**Purpose:** Walk through this with a roofing company owner in ~60 minutes. Score each domain, identify pain points, and leave with a prioritized action plan.

**How to use:** Go domain by domain. Ask the diagnostic questions conversationally â€” don't make it feel like an interrogation. Score as you go. The roadmap builds itself from the scores.

## Domain 1: Lead Generation & Marketing

### Diagnostic Questions
1. Where do your leads come from today? (Storm chasing, referrals, Google, door knocking, paid ads?)
2. What percentage of your revenue comes from storm/insurance work vs. retail?
3. Do you know which zip codes got hit by hail in the last 30 days?
4. How much do you spend on marketing monthly? Do you know your cost per lead?
5. Do you have a website? Does it rank for "[city] roofing contractor"?
6. When a storm hits, how fast do you mobilize vs. competitors?
7. Do you run Google Local Service Ads or Google Business Profile actively?

### Common Pain Points
- No system to detect storms â€” relying on word of mouth or physically driving neighborhoods
- Website is a basic template that doesn't convert or rank locally
- No idea which marketing channels actually produce jobs (not just leads)
- Competitors beat them to storm-hit neighborhoods by days
- Referral program exists in theory but isn't systematized

### Automation Opportunities
- **ðŸ”— Storm Monitor** â€” Automated hail/wind alerts by zip code. Know before competitors. Auto-generate door-knocking routes for affected neighborhoods.
- **ðŸ”— Site Generator** â€” SEO-optimized roofing site with city pages, review widgets, and lead capture. Deployed in days, not months.
- Lead source tracking with unique phone numbers per channel
- Automated Google Business Profile posting (project photos, offers)
- Neighborhood canvassing app with GPS tracking and follow-up triggers

### Quick Wins (Days)
- Deploy Storm Monitor for their service areas
- Set up Google Business Profile optimization
- Launch a basic referral tracking system (even a shared spreadsheet with follow-up reminders)

### Long-Term (Months)
- Full SEO site with city/service pages ranking organically

## Domain 3: Scheduling & Dispatch

### Diagnostic Questions
1. How do you schedule jobs? (Calendar, whiteboard, text messages, memory?)
2. How do crews know where to go tomorrow?
3. What happens when weather cancels a job day-of?
4. How do you handle multi-day jobs and crew transitions?
5. Can your office staff see where crews are right now?
6. How often do scheduling conflicts or double-bookings happen?

### Common Pain Points
- Owner texts crews every morning with addresses
- Weather cancellations create a cascade of rescheduling chaos
- No visibility into crew locations â€” "Where's the crew?" calls all day
- Homeowner doesn't know when the crew is coming (or that they're delayed)
- Material delivery timing doesn't sync with crew schedule

### Automation Opportunities
- Drag-and-drop scheduling board synced to crew phones
- Weather-aware auto-rescheduling with homeowner notification
- GPS crew tracking with automatic "crew is on the way" texts to homeowners
- Material delivery scheduling tied to job schedule
- Automated day-before confirmation texts to homeowners

### Quick Wins (Days)
- Shared Google Calendar with color-coded crews (free, immediate)
- Automated "your roof is scheduled for tomorrow" SMS to homeowners
- Morning crew text blast with job details + address links

### Long-Term (Months)
- Full dispatch board with drag-and-drop, weather integration, GPS tracking
- Route optimization for inspection days
- Automated rescheduling workflow when weather hits

### Common Tools & Weaknesses
| Tool | Weakness |
|------|----------|
| Group texts | No record, things get lost, crews mute the chat |
| Google Calendar | No crew assignment, no customer notifications |
| JobNimbus/AccuLynx | Have scheduling but clunky for field use |
| Whiteboard in office | Not accessible remotely, one person updates it |
