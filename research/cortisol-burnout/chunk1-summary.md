### 1. Cortisol Is NOT a "Stress Hormone"
The most important reframe: **cortisol is an energy deployment hormone**, not just a stress hormone.
- Primary function: releases glucose into bloodstream
- Directs energy to tissues that need it most, especially the brain
- "Brain energy producing hormone" - neurons need glucose to think and respond

### 2. The Critical Cortisol Rhythm
**The #1 takeaway from the entire episode:**
- **Morning:** Cortisol should be HIGH shortly after waking
- **Evening:** Cortisol should be LOW before and during early sleep

If you get these two things right, everything else (health, wellness, performance) follows.

### 3. Cortisol Is Lipophilic
- Can cross cell membranes (unlike adrenaline)
- Crosses blood-brain barrier easily
- High concentration of cortisol receptors in **hippocampus** (memory center)
- This hippocampus connection explains chronic stress â†’ memory problems

### 4. Burnout Symptoms Pattern
Classic burnout = **cortisol rhythm dysregulation:**
- Low energy in morning
- Low energy all day BUT can't sleep at night
- Feeling "wired and tired"
- Morning anxiety

**All of these can be resolved by fixing cortisol rhythm.**
