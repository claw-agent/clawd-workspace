# Roofing Industry Marketing Insights

## From Hook Agency (Roofing Marketing Specialists)

### What Works for Roofers Under $1M
- **Branded yard signs** at job sites
- **Sharp truck wraps** - mobile billboards
- **Google Business Profile** optimization (critical)
- **Local Facebook groups** - engage, don't spam
- **Post reviews** everywhere
- **Tag partners** - cross-promotion
- **Comment like crazy** - be visible in community

### Scaling Past $2M-$10M
Smart roofing companies build systems with **5+ initiatives firing at once:**
1. SEO + Google Ads (search-focused)
2. Facebook Ads (targeted, well-qualified)
3. Branded jobsite marketing (wraps, signs, tarps)
4. Door-knocking systems around every build
5. Referral pipelines with insurance agents and realtors

### Why Brand Matters
- HomeAdvisor leads = competing on price (race to bottom)
- Your own website leads = people who WANT YOU
- Brand-focused customers are less price-sensitive
- Build an asset, not just buy leads

### Key Metrics That Work
- 600%+ traffic increases year-over-year possible
- 65% increase in monthly qualified organic leads
- 92% increase in qualified organic leads YoY
- First page rankings are critical for local search

### Website Design Musts
1. Clear display of services
2. Trust elements (reviews, certifications, insurance)
3. Easy navigation
4. Mobile-optimized
5. Clear calls-to-action
6. Photo portfolio of work
7. FAQ section

## Social Media Platform Strategy

### Facebook (Primary for homeowners 35-65)
- **Content:** Before/after photos, customer testimonials, community involvement
- **Engagement:** Respond to all comments, join local groups
- **Ads:** Target homeowners by zip code, interests in home improvement
- **Post frequency:** 3-5 times per week

### Instagram (Visual Portfolio)
- **Content:** High-quality before/after, team shots, process videos
- **Stories:** Behind-the-scenes, day-in-the-life
- **Reels:** Quick tips, transformations, satisfying content
- **Hashtags:** Local + industry (#[City]Roofing, #RoofRepair, #StormDamage)
- **Post frequency:** 4-7 times per week

### TikTok (Viral Potential, Younger Homeowners)
- **Content:** Satisfying roof reveals, educational quick tips, "day in the life"
- **Trends:** Participate in relevant trends with roofing twist
- **Sound:** Use trending audio
- **Post frequency:** 1-2 times per day for growth

### YouTube (Long-form Trust Building)
- **Content:** Full roof replacement timelapses, how-to guides, maintenance tips
- **SEO:** Title/description optimization for "[City] roofing" searches
- **Frequency:** 1-4 times per month

### Google Business Profile (LOCAL SEO CRITICAL)
- Complete all fields
- Regular posts (weekly)
- Photo updates from every job
- Respond to ALL reviews
- Q&A section populated
- Service areas defined

## Content Pillars for Roofing

1. **Before/After Transformations** - Most engaging content type
2. **Educational** - Roof maintenance tips, when to repair vs replace
3. **Behind-the-Scenes** - Team, process, tools
4. **Customer Testimonials** - Video preferred, text acceptable
5. **Storm/Seasonal** - Prep tips, damage assessment, insurance help
6. **Community** - Local events, sponsorships, charity work
7. **Employee Spotlights** - Humanize the brand

## Seasonal Marketing Calendar

| Season | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| Spring | Storm damage, inspection season, "winter damage" assessment |
| Summer | Peak installation, beat the heat messaging, maintenance |
| Fall | Winter prep, gutter cleaning, last chance before snow |
| Winter | Emergency repairs, ice dam prevention, planning for spring |

## Review Generation Strategy

1. **Ask at peak satisfaction** - right after job completion
2. **Make it easy** - direct link to Google review page
3. **Follow up** - text/email reminder if no review in 48 hours
4. **Respond to ALL** - especially negative ones professionally
5. **Showcase reviews** - on website, social media, ads

## Lead Gen Funnel

```
Awareness (social, ads, SEO)
    ↓
Interest (website visit, content consumption)
    ↓
Consideration (free inspection offer, quote request)
    ↓
Decision (phone consultation, in-person estimate)
    ↓
Action (contract signing)
    ↓
Advocacy (review, referral)
```
