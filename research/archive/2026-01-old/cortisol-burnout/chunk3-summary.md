### Exercise & Cortisol Adaptation
- **Novel vs familiar exercise:** New forms/intensities of exercise cause bigger cortisol spikes; familiar routines produce smaller, shorter cortisol increases
- **Entrainment:** Exercise provides a powerful "entrainment cue" that reinforces cortisol timing via feedback to the SCN (suprachiasmatic nucleus)
- **Pavlovian cortisol response:** Exercising at the same time (within 2-3 hour window) for 4-6 days creates anticipatory cortisol risesâ€”you get energy BEFORE the workout starts
- **Growth hormone correlation:** Novel exercise that spikes cortisol also spikes growth hormone

### Deliberate Cold Exposure
- **Catecholamine release:** Cold causes epinephrine, norepinephrine release (immediate alertness) + long-lasting dopamine arc
- **Cortisol habituation:** Regular cold exposure (>2x/week) loses its cortisol-boosting effect due to adaptation
- **Gender differences myth:** No evidence cold exposure is worse for women; habitual use normalizes cortisol response in both sexes

### Circadian Dead Zone (Noon-5pm)
- **Light insensitivity window:** Bright light during afternoon CANNOT shift biological clock or spike cortisol
- **Afternoon sun = relaxing:** Getting sunlight mid-day promotes calmness, enhances hormones (testosterone, estrogen), improves mood
- **Evening sensitivity opens:** After ~5-6pm, the cortisol-light pathway reopens and becomes EXTRA sensitive

### Evening Light Danger
- **2 hours post-sundown:** Small amounts of bright light (especially blue/green/white LEDs) can cause big cortisol spikes
- **Opposite of morning:** In evening, you want cortisol droppingâ€”light works against this
- **Overhead lights worse:** Neurons detecting luminance are in lower 2/3 of eyes (viewing upper visual field)

### Grapefruit Details
- **Mechanism:** Inhibits CYP3A4 enzyme that breaks down cortisol
- **Effect:** 25-50% longer cortisol duration
- **Bonus:** Also extends caffeine effectiveness
- **Huberman's protocol:** 1 pink grapefruit late morning (~10:30-11am) before first meal
- **Warning:** People on cortisol medications should avoid (skews dosing)
