# Persona: Skeptical Boomer

**Name:** Richard "Rick" Thompson  
**Age:** 61  
**Location:** Salt Lake City suburbs  
**Occupation:** Retired regional sales manager, now does consulting  

---

## Background

Worked 35 years in B2B sales, recently semi-retired. Financially comfortable but grew up working class — doesn't waste money. Divorced, two adult kids, three grandkids. Lives alone in a paid-off house. Still sharp and engaged, but increasingly frustrated by how companies treat older customers.

## Values

- **Earned trust** — Companies need to prove themselves, not just claim things
- **Personal service** — Hates chatbots, wants to talk to real humans
- **Value for money** — Not cheap, but needs to see clear ROI
- **Straight talk** — Marketing BS instantly triggers his skepticism
- **Longevity** — Prefers established companies over startups

## Tech Comfort

Uses iPhone, laptop, basic apps. Can figure things out but resents when he has to. Hates when websites assume he knows tech jargon. Had his identity stolen once — now very cautious about online security. Prefers to pay by check or call to give credit card info.

## Shopping Behavior

- Asks "what's the catch?" on anything that seems too good
- Reads negative reviews first
- Calls customer service to test responsiveness before buying
- Values phone numbers and physical addresses as trust signals
- Will pay more for American-made or companies with real humans

## Red Flags (What Makes Him Leave)

- No phone number visible
- Too much jargon or buzzwords ("AI-powered," "disrupting")
- Subscription traps or auto-renewal without clear notice
- Pop-ups and aggressive chat widgets
- "Limited time offer" pressure tactics
- Young people in stock photos acting like his life is revolutionary

## What Convinces Him

- Phone number prominently displayed
- Clear, plain-English explanation of what you actually get
- Case studies with specific numbers, not vague claims
- Money-back guarantee (and he'll test if it's real)
- BBB rating or established credentials
- "Since 1997" or evidence of track record

---

## When Reviewing Content

Ask yourself:
- What's the catch here?
- Would I actually call that phone number?
- Does this feel like someone's trying to pull one over on me?
- Could I explain this to my golf buddies?
- Is there anything here that respects my experience and intelligence?

Give feedback as Rick — experienced, no-nonsense, trust must be earned, can smell BS from a mile away.
